Cybersecurity Overview

Cybersecurity involves protecting systems, networks, and data from digital attacks, theft, or damage. It encompasses a wide range of practices and technologies to ensure the safety and integrity of information.

  • Network Security: Protecting networks from intrusions, unauthorized access, and attacks.
  • Information Security: Ensuring the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of data.
  • Application Security: Ensuring that software and applications are free from threats and vulnerabilities.
  • Identity and Access Management (IAM): Ensuring only authorized individuals can access critical systems.
  • Endpoint Security: Securing devices like computers, smartphones, and tablets from threats.
  • Cloud Security: Protecting data and applications stored in cloud environments.
  • Incident Response: Planning and responding to security breaches and attacks.

  • Malware: Malicious software designed to damage or disrupt systems (viruses, worms, ransomware).
  • Phishing: Fraudulent attempts to steal sensitive information through fake emails, websites, or messages.
  • DDoS Attacks (Distributed Denial of Service): Overwhelming a network or server with traffic to disrupt services.
  • SQL Injection: Exploiting vulnerabilities in a web application‚Äôs database to inject malicious code.
  • Man-in-the-Middle (MITM) Attacks: Intercepting and altering communications between two parties.
  • Insider Threats: Employees or trusted individuals misusing access to harm the organization.

üöÄ Cybersecurity Technologies & Tools

  • Firewalls: Network security systems that monitor and control incoming and outgoing network traffic.
  • Antivirus Software: Programs designed to detect and eliminate malware on systems.
  • Encryption: The process of converting information into a secure format that prevents unauthorized access.
  • Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS): Tools that monitor network or system activities for malicious behavior.
  • Virtual Private Network (VPN): A secure connection that protects data during transmission over a public network.
  • Multi-factor Authentication (MFA): An additional layer of security requiring multiple forms of identification for access.
